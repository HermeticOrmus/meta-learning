# Contributing

Thank you for wanting to help others learn how to learn.

## Ways to Contribute

### Add a Master Learner Profile

Found a historical or modern figure with documented, learnable practices?

1. Add their profile to `master-learners.md`
2. Include:
   - Brief bio and what they mastered
   - Their specific learning methods (documented, not assumed)
   - Learnable patterns extracted
   - Actionable exercises

**Requirements**:
- Must have documented evidence of their learning approach
- Focus on patterns that can be applied by others
- Avoid hagiography — extract practical wisdom

### Improve Templates

Make the learning templates more useful:

- Fix unclear instructions
- Add helpful prompts
- Remove unnecessary complexity
- Share what worked for you

### Add Evidence

Link to research supporting claims:

- Prefer peer-reviewed sources
- Include study details (not just conclusions)
- Note limitations and context

### Share Examples

How have you applied these methods?

- Add to `/examples/` directory
- Include your learning plan and results
- Be honest about what worked and didn't

### Fix Issues

- Typos and clarity
- Broken links
- Outdated information

## Guidelines

### Keep It Practical

This is not an academic resource. Every addition should answer: "What can someone DO with this information?"

### Secular and Inclusive

Keep content accessible to everyone. Avoid:
- Religious or spiritual framing
- Culture-specific references without explanation
- Jargon that excludes beginners

### Evidence Over Opinion

When making claims about effectiveness:
- Link to research when available
- Note when something is personal experience
- Acknowledge limitations

### Quality Over Quantity

One excellent resource beats ten mediocre ones. One actionable insight beats ten abstract principles.

## Process

1. **Fork** the repo
2. **Create a branch** (`add-feynman-technique-detail`)
3. **Make your changes**
4. **Test** that everything still makes sense together
5. **Submit a PR** with clear description

## Questions?

Open an issue. We'd rather answer questions than review confused PRs.
