# Quick Start Guide

> Start learning effectively in 10 minutes

---

## Step 1: Choose Your Skill (2 minutes)

Ask yourself three questions:

1. **Does this solve a real problem?**
   Not "looks cool on resume" — what actual problem will this help you solve?

2. **Can I commit 30 min/day for 4-8 weeks?**
   Be honest about your capacity. Better to do 30 min consistently than plan 2 hours and skip.

3. **Will I build something real?**
   Learning without building is entertainment. What will you ship?

**Write your answers**:
```
Skill:
Real problem it solves:
What I'll build:
```

If you can't answer these clearly, pick a different skill.

---

## Step 2: Set Up Your Sprint (5 minutes)

### Create your learning directory

```bash
mkdir -p my-learning/[skill-name]
cd my-learning/[skill-name]
```

### Copy the templates

From this repo:
```bash
cp templates/learning-sprint.md README.md
cp templates/mental-model.md mental-model.md
cp templates/progress-journal.md progress.md
```

### Fill out your README

Open `README.md` and complete these sections:
- **Why I'm Learning This** (be honest)
- **Current Level** and **Target Level**
- **Success Criteria** (3 specific, measurable outcomes)
- **Timeline** (4-12 weeks)

Don't overthink it. You can adjust later.

## Step 4: Establish Your Rhythm

### Daily Practice (30 minutes)

**Same time every day** — this matters more than which time.

The 30-minute session:
1. **Review** (2 min): What did I learn yesterday?
2. **Build** (25 min): Focus on doing, not consuming
3. **Log** (3 min): Update progress.md

### Weekly Review (30 minutes)

Pick one day (Sunday works well):
1. Complete weekly review in progress.md
2. Update mental-model.md with new insights
3. Plan next week's focus

---

## Step 5: Apply the Meta-Patterns

As you learn, practice these patterns from the masters:

### Feynman Technique (When Confused)
1. Try to explain the concept simply
2. Find where your explanation breaks down
3. Go back and learn those specific gaps
4. Repeat until a child could understand

### Franklin Tracking (For Consistency)
- Track every day in progress.md
- Celebrate streaks
- Notice patterns in what works

### Tesla Visualization (For Complex Problems)
- Before coding, close your eyes
- Visualize the solution working
- Walk away and let subconscious process

### Direct Practice (Always)
- 20% consuming (tutorials, docs)
- 80% building (projects, experiments)

## Common First-Week Problems

### "I don't know where to start"
Start with the official documentation's quickstart/tutorial. Then immediately build something not from the tutorial.

### "I'm watching tutorials but not retaining"
Stop watching. Build something. Return to tutorials only when stuck on specific problems.

### "I missed a day and feel behind"
You're not behind. Skip guilt. Just practice today. Consistency > perfection.

### "This is harder than I expected"
Good. That means you're learning. Easy = no growth. Adjust timeline if needed.

### "I'm bored / not motivated"
Either:
1. Your "why" isn't real — find a genuine reason or pick different skill
2. You're not building — start a project immediately
